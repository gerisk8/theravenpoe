My Dear Friend

It gives me great pleasure that you have, evidently, found this letter among these pages in that distant, and rather disturbing future of yours.Not that mine was any better, but nor does it matter as of now. It is indeed an exceeding opportunity. However, it is most unfortunate that it is out of my reach to give you an advice, or a lecture on a particular subject you might be interested in. I am most sorry, that my time for you is so brief. However, here is a little thought that you might find useful. A thousand thanks for your time, and, above all, for the cheering and comforting presence of yours.

Believe only half of what you see and nothing that you hear.

Yours,
Edgar Allan Poe
